Engineering Mathematics I: Mathematical Principles

Civil Engineering

  1. Find the concrete quantities for a 1:3:6 mix using 900 kg total material.
  2. A road rises 4 m over 160 m. Find the gradient as a percentage.
  3. Find the area of a trapezium with parallel sides 1.2 m and 2.0 m and depth 0.75 m.
  4. Find the mean of 18, 21, 20, 19 and 22.
  5. Given y=0.05x+100, find y when x=60.

Apply Algebra and Coordinate Geometry

Civil Engineering

  1. Simplify x⁵/x².
  2. Solve 2x+3=15.
  3. Find the distance between A(40,75) and B(100,155).
  4. Find the gradient of the line through (2,5) and (8,17).
  5. Expand (1+2x)^4 up to the x² term.

Trigonometry and Complex Numbers

Civil Engineering

  1. Find the height of a pole if the distance is 20 m and angle of elevation is 28°.
  2. Use cosine rule for sides 30 m and 42 m with included angle 65°.
  3. Find the bearing when ΔE=60 m and ΔN=80 m.
  4. Convert 5+12i into polar form.
  5. Solve sinθ=0.5 for 0°≤θ≤360°.

Calculus and Differential Methods

Civil Engineering

  1. Differentiate y=4x³-3x²+2x-1.
  2. Find the gradient of y=0.02x²+0.5x+100 at x=30.
  3. Integrate 6x²-4x+3.
  4. Evaluate ∫₀³ x² dx.
  5. Use one Newton-Raphson iteration for f(x)=x²-5 from x₀=2.

Vectors, Matrices and Data Analysis

Civil Engineering

  1. Resolve a 20 kN force at 30° into components.
  2. Write two simultaneous equations in matrix form.
  3. Calculate the mean of 22,25,23,24,26.
  4. Find probability if 4 out of 25 readings are outside tolerance.
  5. Explain why standard deviation matters in concrete testing.
