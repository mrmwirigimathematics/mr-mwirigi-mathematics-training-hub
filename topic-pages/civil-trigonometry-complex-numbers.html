Civil Engineering

Trigonometry and Complex Numbers

Trigonometric ratios, sine rule, cosine rule, bearings, waveforms and complex numbers for civil engineering mathematics.

Professional learner notes

Purpose of the topic

Trigonometry supports heights, slopes, setting out, boundaries and survey geometry. Complex numbers prepare trainees for higher engineering mathematics.

These notes are written as class notes for engineering trainees. They follow the same working standard used in class: state the formula, substitute correctly, compute, attach units, and then explain what the answer means in a civil, building or survey situation.

Section 1

Right-triangle trigonometry

Right-triangle trigonometry is used where one angle is 90 degrees. It links unknown lengths and angles.

Key formulae

\[\sin\theta=\frac{O}{H}\]
\[\cos\theta=\frac{A}{H}\]
\[\tan\theta=\frac{O}{A}\]

Worked example

A wall is observed from 12 m away at angle 32°. Height = 12 tan32° = 7.50 m.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

This estimates heights where direct measurement is difficult.

Section 2

Sine rule and cosine rule

Non-right triangles occur in setting out and boundary work. The sine and cosine rules solve missing sides and angles.

Key formulae

\[\frac{a}{\sin A}=\frac{b}{\sin B}=\frac{c}{\sin C}\]
\[c^2=a^2+b^2-2ab\cos C\]

Worked example

Sides 45 m and 60 m include 70°. c²=45²+60²-2(45)(60)cos70°, so c≈61.47 m.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Used in road reserve triangles and boundary checks.

Section 3

Bearings and direction

Bearings give direction clockwise from north. They are essential in survey lines and setting out.

Key formulae

\[\theta=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\Delta E}{\Delta N}\right)\]

Worked example

If ΔE=45 m and ΔN=70 m, θ=tan⁻¹(45/70)=32.74° from north.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

This gives the whole-circle bearing for a line in the north-east quadrant.

Section 4

Trigonometric identities

Identities simplify expressions and solve trigonometric equations.

Key formulae

\[\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta=1\]
\[\tan\theta=\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\]

Worked example

If sinθ=0.6 in a right triangle, cosθ=sqrt(1-0.6²)=0.8.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Identities support checking trigonometric calculations.

Section 5

Complex numbers

Complex numbers extend real numbers using the imaginary unit. They are written in rectangular or polar form.

Key formulae

\[z=a+bi\]
\[r=\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\]
\[\theta=\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{b}{a}\right)\]

Worked example

For z=3+4i, r=5 and θ=53.13°.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Complex numbers support later work in vectors, waveforms and advanced engineering mathematics.

Practice exercises

  1. Find the height of a pole if the distance is 20 m and angle of elevation is 28°.
  2. Use cosine rule for sides 30 m and 42 m with included angle 65°.
  3. Find the bearing when ΔE=60 m and ΔN=80 m.
  4. Convert 5+12i into polar form.
  5. Solve sinθ=0.5 for 0°≤θ≤360°.

Summary

By the end of this topic, a competent trainee should be able to identify the correct formula, perform the calculation accurately, use correct units, and explain the result in a real engineering context.
