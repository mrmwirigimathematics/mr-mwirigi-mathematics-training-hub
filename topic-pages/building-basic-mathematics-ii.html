Section 1

Plane geometry

Rectangles, triangles, trapezia and circles.

In building technology, this concept supports technical judgement in tasks such as floor slabs. The learner must connect the numerical answer to site, drawing, survey, measurement, or data decisions.

Core formula

\[A=lw\]

Worked method

Floor slab

Calculate concrete volume for a slab 8 m by 5 m by 150 mm thick.

Method: identify the given values, write the correct formula, substitute carefully, simplify, attach units, and interpret the result.

Common errors

  • Using a formula before checking what each symbol means.
  • Mixing units such as mm, cm and m.
  • Rounding too early.
  • Giving a final number without technical interpretation.

Practice

  1. Create and solve one original problem involving floor slabs.
  2. Explain the formula used and define every symbol.
  3. State the answer with units and one technical interpretation.
