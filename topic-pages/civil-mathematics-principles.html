Section 1

Civil measurement arithmetic

Unit conversion, estimation, significant figures and site quantities.

In civil engineering, this concept supports technical judgement in tasks such as concrete mix design. The learner must connect the numerical answer to site, drawing, survey, measurement, or data decisions.

Core formula

\[\mathrm{Percentage}=\frac{\mathrm{Part}}{\mathrm{Whole}}\times100\%\]

Worked method

Concrete batching

A 1:2:4 concrete mix is required for a small slab. If the total dry material is 700 kg, estimate cement, sand and aggregate portions by ratio.

Method: identify the given values, write the correct formula, substitute carefully, simplify, attach units, and interpret the result.

Common errors

  • Using a formula before checking what each symbol means.
  • Mixing units such as mm, cm and m.
  • Rounding too early.
  • Giving a final number without technical interpretation.

Practice

  1. Create and solve one original problem involving concrete mix design.
  2. Explain the formula used and define every symbol.
  3. State the answer with units and one technical interpretation.
