Engineering Mathematics I for Building Technology

Algebra, trigonometry, mensuration, graphs and introductory calculus for construction practice.

Purpose of the topic

Engineering Mathematics I supports roof pitch, stair gradients, excavation volumes, cost curves and introductory rate-of-change calculations.

These notes are written as class notes for engineering trainees. They follow the same working standard used in class: state the formula, substitute correctly, compute, attach units, and then explain what the answer means in a civil, building or survey situation.

Section 1

Algebra for construction formulas

Construction formulas often need rearrangement to find missing dimensions.

Key formulae

\[V=lwh\]
\[h=\frac{V}{lw}\]

Worked example

If V=9 m³, l=10 m and w=6 m, h=9/(10×6)=0.15 m.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Used to check slab thickness.

Section 2

Trigonometry in building

Trigonometry determines roof pitch, staircase slope and diagonal distances.

Key formulae

\[\tan\theta=\frac{\mathrm{rise}}{\mathrm{run}}\]

Worked example

Roof rise 1.8 m and run 4.2 m gives θ=tan⁻¹(1.8/4.2)=23.2°.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Used in roof setting out.

Section 3

Mensuration of solids

Volumes and surface areas support material estimation.

Key formulae

\[V=\frac13Ah\]
\[C=\pi d\]

Worked example

A cone-like pile can be approximated using V=1/3πr²h.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Used in stockpile estimation.

Section 4

Graphs and variation

Graphs connect construction output, time, cost and distance.

Key formulae

\[y=mx+c\]
\[m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\]

Worked example

If labour cost increases from 3000 to 5500 over 5 units, gradient=(5500-3000)/5=500.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Gradient is rate per unit.

Section 5

Introductory calculus

Calculus studies change and accumulation.

Key formulae

\[\frac{d}{dx}(ax^n)=anx^{n-1}\]
\[\int ax^n\,dx=\frac{ax^{n+1}}{n+1}+C\]

Worked example

If C=20x²+100x+500, dC/dx=40x+100.

Method discipline: write the formula first, substitute known values, simplify carefully, attach correct units, and interpret the answer.

Engineering interpretation

Derivative estimates marginal cost.

Practice exercises

  1. Transpose V=lwh for w.
  2. Find roof pitch for rise 2.1 m and run 5 m.
  3. Find volume of a trench 30 m by 0.6 m by 0.9 m.
  4. Find gradient between (2,3000) and (6,5000).
  5. Differentiate y=3x²+4x-7.

Summary

By the end of this topic, a competent trainee should be able to identify the correct formula, perform the calculation accurately, use correct units, and explain the result in a real engineering context.
