Section 1

Indices and standard form

Engineering notation, powers and scientific-unit calculations.

In civil engineering, this concept supports technical judgement in tasks such as control points. The learner must connect the numerical answer to site, drawing, survey, measurement, or data decisions.

Core formula

\[a^m a^n=a^{m+n}\]

Worked method

Control-point distance

Two survey control points have coordinates A(120, 250) and B(180, 330). Find AB.

Method: identify the given values, write the correct formula, substitute carefully, simplify, attach units, and interpret the result.

Common errors

  • Using a formula before checking what each symbol means.
  • Mixing units such as mm, cm and m.
  • Rounding too early.
  • Giving a final number without technical interpretation.

Practice

  1. Create and solve one original problem involving control points.
  2. Explain the formula used and define every symbol.
  3. State the answer with units and one technical interpretation.
